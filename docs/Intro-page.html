Hi Susan, genuinely glad you opened this.

"Even in a field as well-established as automated scoring" - that line from your post stuck with me. Because these systems are making decisions that shape the futures of thousands of students like me and the fairness problem is still wide open.

So I started digging and found that most papers just benchmark one model against another and call it progress. Nobody was really asking whether these systems fail certain students more than others, or whether a score with zero explanation is even worth giving.

Your work on fairness gaps and measurement validity was one of the few things I read that was actually asking those harder questions. So I kept going, read everything I could find, wrote a technical paper on where I think current systems are breaking down, and built a live platform where each essay type gets its own scoring adapter, every score comes with a written justification, a teacher can step in and override before anything gets released, and AI scores alongside human scores get stored to track real-time agreement and feed back into the model.

Would love your thoughts on the paper and the platform. What directions you think are worth exploring further, and whether there are questions I should be asking that I have not thought of yet.
